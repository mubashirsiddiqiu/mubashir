About miswaak

Miswaak (miswak, siwak) is a traditional oral-cleaning stick made from the Salvadora persica tree. Used for centuries, it's known for mechanical cleansing and natural compounds that support oral health.

How to use miswaak

  1. Trim ~1 cm of bark from one end and chew gently to fray the fibers into a brush.
  2. Brush gently across teeth and gums; use like a toothbrush for 2–3 minutes.
  3. Rinse and store dry; re-prepare a fresh end when fibers compress.
Tip: replace or sanitize after illness. Not a replacement for professional dental advice — consult your dentist for major issues.

FAQ

Is miswaak safe?

Yes when sourced from Salvadora persica and used correctly. Avoid very aggressive scrubbing.

Does it replace toothpaste?

Miswaak can be used alone; many users combine it with regular dental check-ups and fluoride if recommended by their dentist.
